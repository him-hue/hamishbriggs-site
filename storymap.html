StoryMap

Structural story development. Build narrative architecture beneath your writing so the story stays in focus.

  • Structured thinking, not writing. StoryMap is the layer beneath the draft: a system for developing story logic, character relationships, and narrative coherence so the story grows tall, not wide.
  • Guided development. Prompted intake helps you define what you know, surface what you don't, and keep the vision sharp as complexity grows. StoryMap asks the questions a great development exec would ask, and never forgets the answers.
  • Medium-agnostic. Film, television, novels, comics, games: StoryMap works with the structure of story itself, unbound by the format it ships in.
